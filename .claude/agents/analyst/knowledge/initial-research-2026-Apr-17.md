# Initial Tamil Nadu Governance Research — 2026-Apr-17

## Key Findings by Domain

### Education (UDISE+ 2024-25)
- Secondary dropout rate: 8.5% (up from 7.7%) — 5-year high
- Primary dropout rate: 2.7% (up from ~0%)
- Upper primary dropout rate: 2.8%
- Education spending: 13.7% of TN budget (national avg: 14.7%)
- ~10 lakh teaching vacancies nationally (SSA schools)
- Sources: dashboard.udiseplus.gov.in, prsindia.org

### Healthcare
- IMR: 7.7 per 1,000 live births (2023-24)
- Hospital beds: 1.07 per 1,000 (WHO recommends 2.7)
- Health spending: 5.0% of budget (national avg: 6.2%)
- Health spending as % of GSDP: less than 1% (2006-2019)
- Sources: tn.gov.in, prsindia.org, data.gov.in

### Economy
- GSDP growth: 11.19% (2024-25, highest in India)
- GSDP: Rs 27.22 lakh crore (2023-24)
- Unemployment: 3.5% (down from 5.1%)
- Fiscal deficit: 3.4% of GSDP
- Revenue deficit: 1.6% of GSDP
- Debt: 26.4% of GSDP
- Electricity subsidy burden: Rs 73,821 crore (2019-2025)
- Sources: mospi.gov.in, prsindia.org, niti.gov.in

### Law & Order (NCRB 2023) — STRONGEST DATA
- Crimes against Dalits: 1,921 cases (up 68% from 1,144 in 2019)
- Dalit atrocity conviction rate: 12.2% (national avg: 31.9%)
- Dalits murdered: 74 in one year
- Dalit women raped: 135 (incl. 100 minors)
- Overall crime rate: 264.9 per lakh
- Case pendency: 83.4%
- Sources: data.gov.in/catalog/crime-india-2023, ncrb.gov.in

### Infrastructure
- Jal Jeevan Mission: 89.81% rural coverage
- 100% schools/anganwadis with piped water
- Capital expenditure: Rs 47,681 crore (up 12%)
- Energy spending: 5.5% of budget (national avg: 4.7%)
- Sources: jaljeevanmission.gov.in, prsindia.org

### Agriculture
- Farmer suicides: 728 (2022), 67 farmers + 564 agri workers (2023)
- 63% irrigation from wells/borewells (unsustainable)
- 31% groundwater units over-exploited
- 35% of farmer suicides linked to water scarcity
- Only 37% farmers aware of PMFBY benefits
- Sources: data.gov.in, pmfby.gov.in, CGWB

### TVK Manifesto Key Pledges
- $1.5 trillion TN economy by 2036
- AI governance integration
- Rs 2,500/month for homemakers
- Rs 4,000 unemployment aid for graduates
- 200 units free electricity
- Anti-corruption, transparent governance
- Centre-left ideology (Ambedkar, Periyar, Kamaraj)

2. **Education collapse** — dropout rates at 5-year highs
3. **Groundwater crisis** — 31% over-exploited, farmers dying
4. **Health underspending** — below national average
5. **Fiscal stress masked by GDP growth** — debt, subsidies, revenue deficit
